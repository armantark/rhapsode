Research + Opus review · pre-implementation proposal

Teach a new line before asking it to survive a drill.

The current coach assigns exactly one exercise to a brand-new line: progressive fading. That decision was coherent as a difficulty ladder, but too coarse as an acquisition model. The best surface is one short, coach-only acquisition card that deliberately moves through study, supported reconstruction, and unaided recall.

Recommendation: replace new → progressive_fading with a composite acquisition card. Do not merely add word bank to a random rotation. A new line should leave acquisition only after a checked, self-rated successful recall; failed first attempts remain acquiring and return with support.
What the old logic was

A mode-difficulty ladder masquerading as learning

newprogressive fading
“maximum support”
learningword bank first
“order only”
review+free / typed / random-start recall

The hidden assumption: one fading card is a sufficient first lesson. In code, any first attempt—clean or failed—also moves the line from new to learning, so “new” currently means “never attempted,” not “successfully acquired.”

What was defensible

Support first was right; the unit of design was wrong

The full-text first stage of fading does provide exposure, and word bank was intentionally placed early because all units are supplied. The mistake was treating those as competing session modes. Initial learning needs an ordered study→retrieval sequence with feedback and a success criterion, not one random technique label.

difficulty without success → supported success → unaided success

Research spine

The evidence supports sequencing and criterion, not mode variety for its own sake

01

Alternating study and test produced strong initial and delayed retention; repeated retrieval after success mattered more than repeated restudy.

Karpicke & Roediger, 2007
02

Harder retrieval helps when it succeeds. “Difficult” is not pedagogically useful when the learner has not encoded enough to retrieve.

Pyc & Rawson, 2009
03

Retrieval format must balance effort with success; direct correct-answer feedback is part of making supported retrieval useful.

Smith & Karpicke, 2014
04

Successive relearning practices to a correct-retrieval criterion, then repeats to criterion in later spaced sessions; distributed successes beat massed overlearning.

Rawson & Dunlosky, 2022

Boundary: these studies do not directly test memorizing Japanese lyrics or epic verse with chip reconstruction. The application is an inference from retrieval-success, feedback, and successive-relearning results. Evidence for interleaving categories does not justify cycling UI modes arbitrarily for the same line.

After acquisition

Make early practice outcome-aware

Again / HardRestore support now: acquisition again, word bank, or fading—not weak-link cold recall.
GoodCue recall next: the learner succeeded but still needs a clean retrieval.
EasyBuild flow: chaining once adjacent lines exist; otherwise cue recall after spacing.
Review+Keep the current variety: random start, typed recall, chaining, meaning recall, cue recall.

The existing mode inventory is already broad. The missing capability is response-contingent sequencing, not another pile of exercise names.

Independent Opus review

Strong convergence, two Codex corrections

✓ Agreed

Composite coach-only acquisition card; study → word bank → lead-in recall; one item surface; keep the manual launcher uncluttered.

△ Refined

Do not hide an early failure behind one eventual grade, and do not invent an unsupported 8–9-token cutoff. Failed recall should remain visible to FSRS and mastery; long lines should use authored chunk grain rather than a magic threshold.

Alternatives considered

Why the composite card wins

REJECT

Keep fading-only

It conflates “show the line once” with acquisition and makes the first experience slow without proving a successful retrieval.

INSUFFICIENT

Add word bank to new

It removes the artificial gate, but the planner still picks one unsequenced mode and moves on. Study, feedback, and exit criterion remain accidental.

SELECTED

Composite acquisition

Deterministically provides encoding, a high-success reconstruction, and transfer to unaided recall while preserving one coherent card surface.

Falsifiable validation

Judge the next spaced recall, not first-card comfort

The primary product metric is the rating on a line’s second session—its first spaced, unaided return—plus time spent acquiring the line. The proposal fails if it does not increase Good/Easy first-return rates or reduce early lapses without materially increasing abandonment or acquisition time.

Manual proof: one never-seen Ancient Greek line and one Japanese line through all phases; verify ruby/gloss/audio, chip behavior, bank-hidden production, check-before-grade, failed-acquisition requeue, and one honest scheduling event per graded attempt.
