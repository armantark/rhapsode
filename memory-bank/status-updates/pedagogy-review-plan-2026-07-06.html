PRE-IMPLEMENTATION PLAN · PEDAGOGY REVIEW

The five weakest parts of Rhapsode's practice loop

Full review of the merged app (backend planning/scheduling, prep, practice UI). The architecture is strong — deterministic practice loop, honest undo, ruled decisions. The weaknesses are concentrated where the drills meet memory science: one scheduling bug that rewards failure, one fading drill built backwards, and three smaller alignment gaps.

Scope check: every fix below respects the recorded grill rulings (Anki labels, ladder semantics, neutral peek, 0.9 retention). Nothing here touches the LLM, models, or API signatures.
Critical · scheduling

"Hard" (errors in recall) schedules as a pass in FSRS

The button hint says "Hard — Errors in recall", and everywhere else the app treats it as a failure: it flags the segment difficult, resets/demotes the mastery ladder, and counts in weak-link analytics. But RATING_MAP sends it to FSRS as Rating.Hard — a successful recall. Stability grows, the interval extends, and the line you just recited wrong drifts out of the review queue exactly when it needs reps.

The two failure kinds stay distinguishable in the ladder and analytics (Again wipes the streak; Hard demotes one step — ruling B2 untouched). Only the schedule now agrees that errors are a lapse. For verbatim recitation, an error is a failed card.

High · drill design

Progressive fading hides the line's opening first

The app's own doctrine (_lead_in): recitation is chained, and the strongest cue is the line's opening. Yet fading removes the opening first — stage 2 demands the hardest act (produce the opening with zero cue) while handing you the tail to read. The ramp is inverted.

Before · support fades from the front
arma virumque cano •••• virumque cano •••• •••••••• cano •••• •••••••• ••••
After · support fades toward the cue
arma virumque cano arma virumque •••• arma •••••••• •••• •••• •••••••• ••••

Difficulty now ramps monotonically, and the last supported stage (opening only) is exactly the cue_recall card the ladder graduates to. Japanese token fading mirrors the same direction.

High · session planning

Smart-session triage is due-blind

Triage ranks weak links → learning → new → maintenance, but never consults due_at. An overdue durable line (memory decaying now) ranks below introducing brand-new material, and when the 12-item cap bites, not-yet-due lines can crowd out due ones. Spaced repetition's entire point is being due-aware.

Before
weak→learning→new→all review/durable (due or not)
After
weak→learning→due review/durable→new→not-due maintenance

Anki's rule, applied: repair, then push, then reviews before new, then maintenance only with leftover room.

Medium · drill design

Juncture fading cards drill the head with no anchor

A juncture exists to bind tail of line N → head of line N+1. Recall modes anchor correctly (the tail is the lead-in), but the fading card shows only the head — by the final stage you're reciting "the next line's opening" with no indication of which transition you're crossing. The association being trained isn't on the card.

Before
Recite the next line's opening… ••• •••••• ••
After
… πολλὰς δ᾽ ἰφθίμους Recite the next line's opening… ••• •••••• ••

The fading prompt now carries the juncture's cue (the previous line's tail) as a persistent lead-in, so the transition is practiced as a transition at every stage.

Medium · self-assessment honesty

Recall cards can be graded without ever checking the answer

The UI already says "Recite from memory, then press Space to check before grading" — but nothing enforces it. In Anki you cannot grade before seeing the answer, because self-assessment without verification inflates grades: verbatim errors are precisely the ones the reciter doesn't hear. Fix: on recall modes (cue recall, random start, weak link, chaining, full passage) the grade bar stays disabled until the answer is shown. The peek stays neutral (ruling untouched) — checking just becomes the required step it was always described as. Fading and shadowing cards, where the text is on screen, are unaffected.
